Matthew verse card · cross-dataset

For any Matt verse: Mark parallel, Luke parallel, and Markan-mask status — three datasets at once

This page is the one place that joins all three paired corpora at the verse level. For every verse of Matthew (1,071 in SBLGNT, 1,068 used by the masking pipeline), we look up the best parallel in Mark (from mm), the best parallel in Luke (from ml), and the Markan-mask status from mld. The mask is where things get interesting: if Matthew has a Luke parallel but the masking algorithm removes the Matthew verse from the Q-core, that tells you the pipeline judges Mark a more plausible source than Q for that verse — and lets you interrogate that judgment verse-by-verse.

What this view reveals

The "masked-but-Q" set

Matt verses that have a strong Luke parallel and were removed by the Markan-mask. These are the verses where the three-way pipeline is implicitly saying: "this isn't Q, this is a Markan echo that Matthew and Luke both preserved." For these verses you can read all three Greek texts side by side and form your own view.

The "Q-core surviving" set

Matt verses that are unmasked and paired in the masked primary chain (mld). These pairs are the narrowest possible Q core by construction — the double-tradition remainder after subtracting Markan-looking material from both sides. The exact count (— pairs under the default medium regime) updates live from the bundle.

The "pure Q candidate" set

Strong Luke parallel, no Mark parallel, unmasked. Traditional Q territory — the Sermon on the Mount, Beatitudes, Lord's Prayer, mission discourse, woes on the Pharisees, eschatological discourse. Counts depend on threshold; the default (0.40) is generous.

The "unmasked-but-unpaired" set

Verses the mask did not remove, but the masked primary chain does not pair — either because nothing in Luke is close enough, or because the closest match conflicts with chain monotonicity. Often these are the hardest edge cases: sayings with minor Matthean expansion that leaves a high surface-divergence.
