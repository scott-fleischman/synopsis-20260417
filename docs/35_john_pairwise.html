John ↔ Synoptic pairwise

anchor-level shared tradition · not a continuous chain

Three first-class pairwise dashboards — John↔Mark, John↔Matthew, John↔Luke. The supplement's core methodological point is on display here: you cannot run a Synoptic-style narrative-alignment chain on John and one other Gospel and expect it to work. John is too transformational for a monotonic lexical backbone. The right model is anchor plus low-verbatim pairwise: shared narrative episodes are curated as anchors, the full verse-matrix is computed anyway (580K–1M rows per pair) so local lexical candidates are visible, and the four-way burden ledger (John-uses-Syn, Syn-uses-John, shared tradition, independent convergence) is the primary interpretive tool. Each anchor is then scored independently and the burdens summed per pair, so no single anchor can carry the ranking.

What the headline numbers are not. Don't read "33 candidate pairs" as "33 instances of dependence." Many are brief/formulaic agreements that the chain models would dismiss. The interpretive weight sits on the anchor episodes — 10 per pair for Mark/Matt, 9 for Luke — where narrative content overlaps even when wording does not. The strongest single lexical case across all three pairs is John 12:8 ~ Matt 26:11 at score 0.8571 (6-token exact run) — Matthew's anointing-at-Bethany passage matches John's almost verbatim, a result that would be indistinguishable from direct dependence if we did not also see the same saying echoed in Mark. Per-anchor aggregate: shared-anchor-tradition wins at 9 of 9 Luke anchors, 10 of 10 Mark anchors, and 10 of 10 Matt anchors — the ranking holds anchor by anchor, not only at the global level.
