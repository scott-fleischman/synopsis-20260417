Gospel Dependence & Early Christian Intertextuality Workbench

A layered, auditable investigation of literary dependence across the four canonical Gospels, the Gospel of Thomas, the New Testament letters, and a select inventory of early-Christian apocrypha. Built from the SBLGNT Greek text and apparatus, MorphGNT morphology, Coptic Scriptorium Thomas, and M. R. James's apocryphal inventory. Every layer is a separate evidence stream — retrieval scores, burden ledgers, sensitivity grids, negative-space controls — and every intermediate artifact is hash-audited at source.

This workbench does not settle the direction of dependence. It quantifies what each direction-hypothesis must explain, and flags shared Old Testament/liturgical formulas so they do not masquerade as Gospel dependence. The Conclusions page is the only surface that makes directional claims; every other page is neutral evidence.

Seven research questions this workbench answers

Each question has a distinct evidence shape. The backbone questions (Matthew/Mark, Matthew/Luke) can be posed as order-sensitive literary-dependence questions. The sayings questions (Thomas, Q, James) need a network model. The Johannine question needs anchor-level transformation analysis. Treating them with one pipeline would over-commit on the easy cases and under-detect on the hard ones.
