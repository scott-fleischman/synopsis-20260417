Thomas × canonical matrix

116 Coptic logia vs Matt / Mark / Luke / John · 46 curated parallels · logion-level dossiers

All 116 Thomas units from NHC II,2 plotted against the four canonical gospels. The logion-by-Gospel matrix is the authoritative view in the 2026-04-18b supplement: dependence is assessed per saying, not as a corpus-level yes/no claim. Each row shows relation type, matrix strength, and direct-dependence burden. 46 of the 116 logia have a curated canonical parallel; the remaining 70 rows remain visible to show the corpus-level sparsity of Thomas's contact with the canon.

Methodological note (h18b method). Thomas is treated logion-by-logion, not as a single corpus that either does or does not depend on the Synoptics. Automatic Coptic→Greek dependence scoring is not implemented, so the matrix is curated evidence augmented by lemma/motif signal — long exact Coptic-Greek runs do not exist by construction. Relation types separate possible Synoptic influence (4 logia) from shared sayings tradition (14) from overlapping Jesus tradition of uncertain direction (28).
For single-logion reading alongside Greek canonical text, use 21 Thomas parallels. For the role of Thomas within the evidence network, use 18 intertext network and 31 canonical Gospel square.
