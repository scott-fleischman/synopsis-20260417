System hypothesis space

five system-level models · pairwise burden comparison · cross-links to per-direction dossiers

Pairwise direction registries (page 36) tell you what one direction must explain. System models tell you what coherent packages of directions claim collectively. This page documents the five canonical system models actively debated for the Synoptics and John, each with its assumptions, must-explain obligations, contrary evidence, and the best response available if one still wants to hold the model together.

How the five relate. Two-source, Farrer, and Griesbach/two-gospel are three mutually-exclusive accounts of Matthew↔Luke↔Mark. Proto-Mark relaxes the assumption that canonical Mark is the Mark-like source. John shared-anchor tradition addresses the John↔Synoptic question without presupposing direct Gospel-to-Gospel use. The same burden scores on page 36 evaluate pairwise pieces of these models — see the burden comparison below.
Sources: 04_system_hypothesis_space.yaml, 03_direction_burden_summary.csv, 62_directional_conclusion_ranking_and_missing_key_directions.yaml.
