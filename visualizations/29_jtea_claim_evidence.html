Claim → evidence linkage

Low-verbatim corpus (John, Thomas, epistles, apocrypha) — each claim mapped to its supporting artifacts and three-way confidence.

The reproducibility-patch rerun splits confidence into three dimensions — retrieval (is the automatic similarity signal there?), philological (does close reading sustain the case?), and formula risk (is the lexical overlap a scriptural citation or stock liturgical formula that might inflate the apparent connection?). A case is only strong when all three dimensions are favorable. This page is the authoritative register mapping each interpretive claim to the artifacts (CSV rows, YAML records, case studies) that support it, with provisional_best_explanation spelled out.

Formula-risk flagging across the retrieval layers

Exact Greek runs of length ≥4 are first retrieved automatically. The rerun then flags each candidate for formula risk: shared scripture quotations, liturgical boilerplate, and common prayer fragments fall in the "high" bucket and should not be taken as evidence of direct literary dependence between the epistle and gospel on their own.
