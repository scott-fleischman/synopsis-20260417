Concept signatures

Cross-corpus motif clusters spanning Gospels, epistles, and Thomas

A concept signature is a thematic cluster that appears across two or more corpus layers without necessarily showing strong verbal agreement — the kind of case where the automatic retrieval layer scores poorly but a reader immediately recognizes the saying. Each signature below carries four burden scores: independence (how hard it is to argue these cases arose independently), direct literary (dependence on a specific written text), oral tradition (stable gist transmitted orally), and common written source (shared lost written tradition). The relative weighting of these four is itself the diagnostic — e.g. worker_wages shows very_high independence burden because the verbal match (ὁ ἐργάτης τοῦ μισθοῦ αὐτοῦ) is too specific to arise twice by accident.
