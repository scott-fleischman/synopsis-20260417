System-model scorecard

13 models · 5 explanatory areas · required directions · burden sums · strength/burden cards

Each system-level model of the Synoptic Problem has to carry a specific set of required directions. This page shows all thirteen atlas models side by side: the required directions (clickable, each drills into the per-direction dossier), the sum of the selected direction burdens where computable, a five-area fit matrix (Mark–Matthew, Mark–Luke, Matthew–Luke double tradition, minor agreements, John anchors), and the strongest support and heaviest remaining burden under that model.

Models are grouped by family. Within each family, rows are sorted by burden sum ascending — lowest-burden model first, with a highlight on the best-in-family card. Burden sums are summed from published direction burdens, not probabilities.

How to read this page: a lower sum_selected_burden_scores_available means the direction set under this model has accumulated less unexplained material in the current ledgers — not that the model is closer to truth. A model can carry additional non-burden-scored obligations (Q reconstruction, lost-source postulates, de-Johannization). The five-area fit labels are the atlas's qualitative reads of how well the current evidence sits with the model in that area; not_applicable marks areas a model does not claim to resolve.
