Gospel Square

every pairwise Gospel relationship · methods calibrated to the phenomenon

A single matrix view of every pairwise Gospel relationship the workbench covers. Different pairs use different methods — a point the supplement makes explicit: a Synoptic-style chain model is the right tool for Mark↔Matthew and Mark↔Luke, but it is the wrong tool for John↔anything, where the evidence is anchor-level transformation rather than continuous lexical alignment. Each off-diagonal cell shows the count the method actually produces, with a confidence chip calibrated to the method's reach.

Seven directed cells have first-class data. The diagonal carries self-stats only. Mark↔Luke and the three John pairwise cells are new with the 2026-04-18b supplement; cells marked NEW reflect that.
Pair → direction → system. This square is the pairwise view of evidence. The model-atlas companion view 40 · Gospel direction matrix relabels the same 4×4 as directional hypotheses (16 ordered source→target claims) with best-response models and available burden scores. Page 39 shows the 13 system-level models that combine those directions (Markan priority, two-source / Q, Farrer, Griesbach, Augustinian, proto-Mark, oral-tradition, Johannine anchor-tradition, etc.).
